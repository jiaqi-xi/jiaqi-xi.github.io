    Case: Taking max

    Motivation: How to compute the gradient of L(θ)=argmax⁡i{xi(θ)∣i=1,⋯ ,K}L(\theta)=\underset{i}{\operatorname{argmax}}\left\{x_{i}(\theta) \mid i=1, \cdots, K\right\}L(θ)=iargmax​{xi​(θ)∣i=1,⋯,K}?

    πτ,i′=exp⁡(xi′/τ)∑k=1Kexp⁡(xk′/τ)\pi_{\tau, i}^{\prime}=\frac{\exp \left(x_{i}^{\prime} / \tau\right)}{\sum_{k=1}^{K} \exp \left(x_{k}^{\prime} / \tau\right)} πτ,i′​=∑k=1K​exp(xk′​/τ)exp(xi′​/τ)​

    Here, τ\tauτ is a hyper-parameter called temperature. The smaller τ\tauτ is, the closer the softmax approximates one-hot vector.
